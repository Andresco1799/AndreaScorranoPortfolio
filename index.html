MicroBioma Information Design

Filming and editing by Andrea Scorrano

2025

Information Design

University project ‚Äì Information Design lab

Supervision by Marco Luitprandi and Giorgio Uboldi

Microbioma is an interactive installation project that explores the correlation between human activities and aquatic micro-ecosystems, with a particular focus on the role of phytoplankton as an indicator of water quality in rivers and streams. The aim of the project is to raise public awareness of the importance of these microscopic organisms, often overlooked but fundamental for ecological balance and the health of the entire aquatic system. Through a visual and sensory narrative, the installation translates scientific concepts into an experiential language, fostering critical awareness of the environmental consequences linked to everyday behaviors.

The Microbioma project is based on research aimed at understanding the ecological processes that influence the balance of aquatic systems, with particular attention to the role of phytoplankton as a biological indicator of water quality. This microscopic component plays a fundamental role in the food chain and in regulating the oxygenation of rivers and streams, while being extremely sensitive to environmental changes such as pH levels, nutrient concentrations, and the presence of pollutants.

To establish a solid scientific foundation, the project drew on data related to the decrease in pH and the absorption of CO‚ÇÇ by the oceans, both key processes in understanding water acidification. In addition, it investigated the mechanisms of eutrophication, a phenomenon characterized by excessive phytoplankton growth caused by an overabundance of nutrients, which can lead to severe ecological imbalances.

The installation is structured into three main areas: an introductory section that highlights the ecological importance of phytoplankton; an experiential section that engages visitors in a process made of choice, action, and consequence; and a third area dedicated to scientific exploration, where graphs and data illustrate environmental challenges and strategies for balance. The project‚Äôs target audience is university students, chosen for their ability to grasp complex content and transform it into critical thinking and active awareness.

The exhibition of the installation represented an important moment of dialogue with the public. The primary target audience, university students, was fully reached: around 20 people took part in the experience, including three international students, confirming the project‚Äôs ability to communicate in an immediate and accessible way. The feedback received was positive, both in terms of content and interaction. In particular, the immersive format was highly appreciated, as well as the way complex topics such as the balance of phytoplankton were made understandable through a visual and symbolic language.

HumusCene Data Visualization
Virtual Reality

2024

Data Visualization, Virtual Reality (VR)

University project - Design Culture 2024

Supervision by Roberto Saponelli

Humuscene is a speculative 3D data visualization experienced through a VR headset. It explores how environments might transform over thousands of years under the influence of climate change, blending deep-time thinking with posthuman ecologies.Based on a photogrammetric scan of the cloister of the Monastery of Santa Chiara in San Marino, captured using WEBINAR technology, the project generates a precise point cloud that becomes the foundation for an imagined, stratified future. Through immersive, gamified interaction, Humuscene redefines data as a space of experiential speculation, where users navigate evolving landscapes shaped by decay, growth, and synthetic matter. Humuscene‚Äôs virtual reality experience presents speculative data in a way that feels both virtual and physical. Rather than relying solely on graphic visualizations, the goal was to represent data as intuitively as possible, allowing users to immediately perceive the speculative consequences of climate change.

Rather than relying solely on graphic visualizations, the goal was to represent data as intuitively as possible, allowing users to immediately perceive the speculative consequences of climate change.

The process began with the creation of a photogrammetry of the site, which was cleaned and processed to serve as the foundation for the virtual environment. The space was then divided into three distinct areas: a data projection for the year 2150, one for 2500, and a more speculative projection for the year 3000. For each of these spaces, procedural animations were developed to emphasize the growth of fungi and invasive species reclaiming the environment. These procedural animations were designed to be adaptable to any location. Additionally, atmospheric effects were incorporated to enhance the realistic and speculative quality of the scenes, drawing inspiration from the visual language of post-apocalyptic video games.

Butterfly Connection Data Visualization
Web Design

2025

Data Visualization, Web Design

University Project

Supervision by Giorgio Uboldi

Website: ButteflConnection

This project offers an interactive and comprehensive visualization of the relationships between butterfly species observed in the Veneto region and their natural predators. The data supporting this work have been meticulously gathered from reputable sources such as GloBI, iNaturalist, and the IUCN Red List, and the entire visualization is grounded in a reanalysis of a scientific article documenting butterfly biodiversity within the area. Through this platform, users can dynamically explore species interactions, gaining deeper insights into the ecological complexity and interdependencies among different insect groups in this specific geographic context.

From a technical perspective, the project was developed using JavaScript and D3.js to construct an interactive graph composed of three distinct levels: the complete network displaying all species and their interconnections, a focused view showcasing only butterfly species, and a dedicated layer highlighting their predators. This multi-layered structure enables seamless navigation among different ecological scopes, enhanced with zooming, panning, and search filtering capabilities. Each node represents a species enriched with detailed information accessible via user interaction, while the edges reveal the ecological relationships studied. This structured approach delivers a clear, functional overview of natural balances within the Veneto territory, combining scientific rigor with modern web-based visualization techniques.

The Pulper Reject Research and Product Design

2023

Research and sperimental Product Design

University Project - Thesis Project

Development of a Noise Barrier from an industrial waste

Supervision by Simone Secchi

This thesis project stems from the challenge of addressing an unusual theme within the field of Design: to give a design application and productive purpose to Pulper waste, a residue from the industrial paper-making process. The research originated from a personal interest in environmental issues and Ecodesign, focusing on the study of materials and the production processes they entail. The initial phase explored waste paper recycling and its impact, with the aim of understanding how Pulper is generated and identifying the main factors behind it: cellulose-based composite products and technological advancements in paper processing. The findings revealed that disposal and incineration remain the most common solutions, highlighting the need to envision new opportunities for its reuse.

The second phase of the project concentrated on the polymeric fraction of Pulper waste, which represents the majority of its composition. Collaboration with companies specializing in the recovery of this material provided samples and technical insights that informed possible design applications. The research concluded that the most suitable field of use would be large-scale outdoor products, with particular relevance in the design of sound-absorbing highway barriers. This final proposal not only reimagines Pulper as a valuable material but also gives it a new identity, combining environmental sustainability with functional and aesthetic qualities, transforming waste into a resource for design.

Il Palazzo, Il Quartiere, la Lavanderia Film Making & Sound Design

2024

Film Making, Sound Design

University Project - Digital Narrative Lab

Supervision by Stefano Luca and Pietro Maria De Tilla

The project originates from the observation of the coin laundry at the Grattacielo in Rimini, a transitional space that mirrors a fragmented and silent community. The directing approach embraced a detached, almost mechanical gaze, composed of fixed shots and horizontal pans that slowly scan the interior. This repetition reflects the monotony of gestures and the suspended atmosphere of the laundromat, while occasional close-up details interrupt the visual logic to reveal unexpected fragments of intimacy. The formal choices highlight the anonymity and repetitiveness of the space, turning it into a lens through which everyday dynamics can be observed.

At the same time, sound design played a crucial role in shaping the immersive dimension of the work. By analyzing the components of a washing machine and its 30-minute cycle, the key sonic moments of the process were identified. Each component was paired with one or more foley sounds, recorded in the urban environment surrounding the laundromat‚Äîstreet noises, voices, traces of daily life. These sounds were then re-composed and orchestrated to create a new product sound, which does not simply replicate the machine but instead amplifies its perception, merging reality with abstraction.

The short film documents the neighborhood at the foot of the Grattacielo through the laundromat, emphasizing its role as a space of transit and observation. Both interior and exterior shots were taken from a fixed vantage point, limited to horizontal pans across the two main rooms. This directorial approach established a consistent and repetitive shooting method, designed to highlight the functional and standardized qualities of the environment.

Heartease Product & Physical Computing

2024

Product and Physical Computing

University Project - Product Design Lab

Supervision by Mirko Piccin and Daniela Maurer

HeartEase is a human-centered yet technologically advanced device designed to alleviate non-cognitive symptoms such as stress in people affected by Alzheimer‚Äôs disease. Its familiar, heart-like form reduces feelings of detachment and creates a welcoming, reassuring presence for patients. At the same time, it actively involves caregivers by monitoring stressful moments and signaling when support may be needed. Through gentle, non-invasive sensory stimulation, HeartEase aims to improve overall well-being while fostering a calmer and more supportive environment.

The object activates after being lifted: light and sound guide the breathing.

Shooting by Andrea Scorrano

Heartsease was showcased at the event "Care is an Idea", held at the Teatro Concordia in San Marino. The meeting brought together experts from the healthcare and academic sectors to discuss the topic of dementia.

The system is composed of three interconnected elements: the Tracker, the Helper, and the App. The wearable Tracker detects states of stress through physiological signals and communicates them in real time to the Helper device. The Helper provides calming feedback through light and sound, and can guide patients in breathing exercises by inflating and deflating its soft, elastic shell to simulate a slow, regular rhythm. Meanwhile, the App enables caregivers to track episodes of stress, annotate contextual information, and recognize behavioral patterns to design personalized strategies for care.

The prototype was developed by combining digital fabrication with repurposed medical components. A 3D-printed shell, coated in liquid latex, gives the Helper its final form while allowing flexibility during inflation. The internal system uses a motor and solenoid valve, adapted from a blood pressure monitor, and controlled through an Arduino Nano. Stress levels are monitored via the open-source smartwatch Bangle.js, which communicates with the system through a custom app built on AppInventor. Together, these elements create a simple yet effective network of devices to enhance relaxation and strengthen the connection between patient and caregiver.
